📖 Reading — "Food Around the World" 10–15 min

Food Around the World

Food is amazing! People eat different things in different countries. I love learning about food from around the world.

My name is Emma and I love food. My favourite food is pasta. I eat pasta at least three times a week! I really like tomato pasta and cheese pasta. I also love pizza — especially with mushrooms and olives on top.

But I don't like everything. I don't like spicy food at all. When I eat something spicy, my eyes water and my face goes red! I also don't like fish very much. My mum loves fish, but I think it smells funny.

My friend Maya loves different food. She likes rice and chicken, and she loves plantain — it's a type of banana that you cook. Maya doesn't like cheese. She says it's too strong. We always laugh about this because I love cheese and she hates it!

In Japan, people eat a lot of sushi and rice. Sushi is raw fish with rice. Some people love it, some people hate it! In Mexico, people eat tacos — they are delicious! In India, people love curry with spices. In France, people love bread and cheese.

I think the best thing about food is that everyone is different. Some people love sweet things, some people prefer salty or spicy food. What about you? What do you love? What don't you like? Food is a great way to learn about other cultures and countries!

1. What is Emma's favourite food?
2. What does Maya love to eat?
3. Why doesn't Emma like spicy food?
4. What is plantain?
🔍 5. Inference: Why do Emma and Maya "always laugh" about cheese?
📖 6. Vocabulary: Find a word in the text that means "not cooked" (as in raw fish).
💬 7. Your turn: Which country's food from the text would you most like to try? Why?

Reading notes
🔧 Grammar — I like / I don't like / Do you like…? 10–15 min

Visual: two ways to use "like"

Pattern 1 — Like + noun (a thing)
I like pizza.
I don't like spinach.
She likes pasta. (-s!)
He doesn't like eggs.
Pattern 2 — Like + verb-ing (an activity)
I like swimming.
I like dancing.
I don't like waiting.
Verb + -ing = "I enjoy doing this"

Question: Do you like chocolate? → Yes, I do. / No, I don't.

He/She: Does she like pasta? → Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.

Add the Intensifier 💪

Fill in the blank with the best intensifier. Look at the scale below!

weak strong
quite really absolutely

1. "I ___ love chocolate."  (really / absolutely)

2. "She ___ doesn't like spicy food."  (really / absolutely)

3. "I ___ like salad — it's OK."  (quite / a bit)

Write Your Own ✍️

Write a restaurant review! What do you love? What don't you like?
Use: I love…, I really like…, I don't like…, I absolutely hate…

Example: I love the pizza here! I don't like the salad.
