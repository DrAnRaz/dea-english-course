Classroom Bingo! 🎯🏫
A vocabulary game about classroom objects · Dea's English · Session 3

How to Play

  1. Print ONE bingo card for each player (Cards A, B, C, or D — each player gets a DIFFERENT card).
  2. Print the Caller List on a separate page for the teacher.
  3. The teacher reads sentences from the list OUT LOUD — one at a time.
  4. Find the matching object on your bingo card and mark it with a coin or bingo chip.
  5. When you have 4 in a row (across ➡️, down ⬇️, or diagonal ↗️), shout "BINGO!"
  6. IMPORTANT: To WIN, you must say one full sentence about an object in your winning row!
    Example: "There is a whiteboard in our classroom." ✓
💡 Tips for the teacher: Listen carefully! Mark only one item per sentence. You may repeat a sentence once if asked. The game ends when the first player correctly wins — or keep playing until all players win!

Caller List TEACHER ONLY
Read each sentence aloud — one at a time. Pause between sentences.

Tip: Read slowly and clearly. You may repeat a sentence once if asked. Tick each sentence as you read it.

  • 1"There is a whiteboard at the front of the classroom."
  • 2"Each student has a desk."
  • 3"Everyone sits on a chair."
  • 4"Maya has a pencil case on her desk."
  • 5"She writes with a pen."
  • 6"Emma uses a pencil to draw."
  • 7"There is a ruler in her pencil case."
  • 8"She has an eraser for corrections."
  • 9"There is a book on every desk."
  • 10"There is a big bookshelf at the back."
  • 11"The teacher has a computer."
  • 12"There is a plant next to the teacher's desk."
  • 13"There are colourful posters on the wall."
  • 14"There is a world map on the wall."
  • 15"There are windows in the classroom."
  • 16"There is a door at the back of the room."
  • 17"There is a bin next to the teacher's desk."
  • 18"There is a clock on the wall above the whiteboard."
  • 19"Students put their bags under their desks."
  • 20"There is a globe on the bookshelf."
When a player shouts BINGO: Ask them to read one full sentence about an object in their winning row. Example: "There is a whiteboard in our classroom." — If correct, they WIN!
Bingo Chips — Cut Out & Use! ✂️
Cut out the circles and use as markers. Each colour = one player.
Alternative: Use coins, small stones, pasta pieces, or torn paper — anything works! You need about 16 markers per player.
