📖 Reading — "A Day in the Life of Sara" 10–15 min
A Day in the Life of Sara

Sara is eleven years old. She lives with her family in Bristol, England. Here is what Sara does every day.

Sara wakes up at seven o'clock in the morning. She gets up, washes her face, and gets dressed quickly. Then she goes downstairs and has breakfast with her mum. She usually eats toast with jam and drinks orange juice. Sara's mum always drinks coffee.

Sara starts school at eight thirty. She walks to school with her friend Amy. They talk about their favourite TV shows on the way. Sara loves science and she is good at maths, but she doesn't like history very much.

At lunchtime, Sara eats in the school canteen with her friends. She usually has a sandwich and a piece of fruit. After lunch, they play in the playground for twenty minutes.

Sara finishes school at three thirty. She goes home and has a snack — usually an apple or some biscuits. Then she does her homework. She sits at her desk and works quietly for about an hour.

In the evening, Sara has dinner with her family at six o'clock. They usually eat pasta or chicken with vegetables. After dinner, she watches TV or reads a book. She never goes to bed late — she sleeps at nine thirty.

On weekends, Sara's routine is different. She wakes up later, at nine o'clock, and she plays football with her friends in the afternoon. She loves weekends!
1. What time does Sara wake up?
2. What does Sara eat for breakfast?
3. What does Sara do after she finishes school?
4. What time does the family have dinner?
🔍 5. Inference: How do we know Sara is a good student?
📖 6. Vocabulary: What does "canteen" mean in the text?
💬 7. Your turn: Is your daily routine similar to Sara's? What is different?

Reading notes
🔧 Grammar — Present Simple: the -s Rule 10–15 min

"When we talk about what ONE person (he/she) does every day, we add -s or -es to the verb. It's a small but important signal!"

Visual: I vs She — spot the difference

I (me, the speaker)
wake up I wake up at 7.
go I go to school.
watch I watch TV.
have I have lunch.
do I do homework.
She / He (someone else)
wakes up She wakes up at 7.
goes He goes to school.
watches She watches TV.
has He has lunch. ⚠️
does She does homework.

Hungarian note: Hungarian doesn't change verb endings for third person — this is new. Reassure Dea: "Just remember: add -s or -es for she/he. That's all!"

🐛 Spot the Mistake

  • "Tom wake up at seven." → wakes up
  • "She have breakfast." → has
  • "I watches TV." → watch
